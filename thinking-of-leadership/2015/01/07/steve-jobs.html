How you can avoid leadership drift

Posted by Alanson Van Fleet | Jan 7 2015 | tags: Steve Jobs leadership passion focus
You have to be burning with an idea, or a problem, or a wrong that you want to right. If you're not passionate enough from the start, you'll never stick it out.
Steve Jobs

Let's face it, Steve Jobs was right. Leadership is tough. Some days just suck and you ask yourself: "Do I really have to make another sales call? Take another two hour conference call? Prepare another deck for the board meeting?

The answer comes down to this. What is my "burning idea?" What "wrong am I trying to set right?" What purpose am I trying to serve?

Answering those questions is the true job of leadership. Without those answers, your leadership can drift and fade into mere routines. I’ve seen it happen, and I’ve felt it happen to me.
